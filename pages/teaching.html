My Teaching Portfolio

I have taught forty graduate and undergraduate courses in my career, including real analysis, ordinary differential equations, partial differential equations, linear algebra, calculus I, II, III, etc. I have taught a variety of class sizes where the largest class had over three hundred students (pre-pandemic). I am a huge supporter of equality, diversity, and inclusion. In fact, I bring a lot of variety with me. I was born in a small town by the river Ganges back in India, a first-generation college student from a low-income group. After coming to the United States, I was fortunate to teach students in Texas, Illinois, Maine, and now in Connecticut. Due to my background, I can relate to the problems that a diverse group of students may present. Most importantly, I can address them using my own journey. This approach often turns into a rewarding experience as I believe in teaching beyond the classroom. My dedication and passion for teaching have been acknowledged by multiple teaching awards. Recently, I was honored with the Dean's recognition for "Excellence in Teaching" for my performance during the $2020-21$ academic year. At Northern Illinois University, I was elected as the ``Most Valuable Professor" in Fall 2014 and Fall 2015, and I was the recipient of the ``Certificate of Teaching Excellence" by the Department of Mathematical Sciences in Spring 2015.
